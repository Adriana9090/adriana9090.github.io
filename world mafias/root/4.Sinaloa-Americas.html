Cartel de Sinaloa

History The Cartel de Sinaloa started out as the Guadalajara cartel, Mexicoâ€™s largest crime organization in the 80s. This organization started as a small group of farmers cultivating marijuana and poppy flowers in northern Mexico. In the 1960s and 70s, there was a transition from production and distribution of marijuana and heroin to transportation of cocaine coming from Colombia. The movement of bulk shipments of cocaine was via airplane and boat to Central America and Mexico, then by land routes into the United States. Estimates of its annual revenue ranged from $3 billion to $39 billion. The Sinaloa Cartel operates in the "Golden Triangle", the states of Sinaloa, Durango, and Chihuahua. The region is a major producer of Mexican opium and marijuana.According to the U.S. Attorney General, the Sinaloa Cartel was responsible for importing into the United States and distributing nearly 200 short tons of cocaine and large amounts of heroin between 1990 and 2008.According to the National Drug Intelligence Center, within the U.S. the Sinaloa Cartel is primarily involved in the distribution of cocaine, heroin, methamphetamine, marijuana and MDMA.It is also the majority supplier of illicit fentanyl to North America.

Key Facts

  • Founded:1988
  • Founding Location: Sinaloa, Mexico
  • Years active: 1988-present
  • Criminal Activities: Money laundry, weapon trafficking, kidnapping, drug trafficking
  • Rivals: Los Zetas, Juarez Cartel, Beltran Leyva Cartel, Tijuana Cartel, Barrio Azteca, Jalisco New Generation Cartel
